Descrption:
A Sprint is a short, fixed time period (1–4 weeks) in which the team works to complete selected tasks and deliver a usable product increment. 
It helps teams focus on achieving a specific goal within a limited time.
=================================



Sprint in Scrum:
A Sprint is a fixed time period (usually 1 to 4 weeks) during which a Scrum team works to complete a set of tasks or user stories from the product backlog. 
It is a short, focused development cycle where the team plans, builds, tests, and delivers a working product increment.
 Each Sprint has a clear goal, and at the end of the Sprint, a usable and potentially shippable product is delivered.
 ===================================

Sprint Timeline and its Importance:
The Sprint timeline defines the structure and sequence of activities that happen during the Sprint, such as Sprint Planning, Daily Scrum meetings, Development work, Sprint Review, and Sprint Retrospective. 
It acts like a roadmap that guides the team on what to do and when to do it within the limited time frame.
==================================

How it helps the development team:
The Sprint timeline helps the team stay organized, focused, and disciplined by providing a clear schedule of tasks and events.
 It improves time management, ensures continuous progress tracking through daily meetings, and helps in identifying and resolving issues quickly. 
 It also promotes teamwork, transparency, and regular feedback, which leads to better productivity and high-quality product delivery.
